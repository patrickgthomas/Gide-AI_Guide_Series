About the Guide

Patrick Thomas’s book, The AI Gold Rush, examines the modern industry of online creators who profit by selling the dream of automated wealth. Using the historical California Gold Rush as a metaphor, the author argues that the most consistent financial gains belong to those selling tools and education rather than those attempting to use the technology for quick riches. The text reveals how sophisticated marketing funnels exploit human psychology by pairing the hope of financial freedom with the fear of being left behind by technical progress. Readers are cautioned against survivorship bias, which amplifies rare success stories while hiding the common reality of failed ventures. Ultimately, the source advocates for a critical approach to incentives, suggesting that the true value of AI lies in its ability to solve concrete problems rather than its potential for passive income. The book concludes that while the technology is transformative, building a durable business still requires creating genuine value for others.

It is written for everyday people, not programmers or technical experts.
